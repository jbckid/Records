SPOTLIGHT ON: MUSEUMS

By Christine Bruzzese

The Metropolitan Museum of Art, Museum of Modern Art and Whitney Museum are just three of the many located in New York City. In all five boroughs you will find a rich assortment of museums for all interests. Here is a sample of publications in the City Hall Library on this topic.

Good Old Modern: an Intimate Portrait of the Museum of Modern Art by Russell Lynes is a history of this museum from its founding in 1929 until 1973 when the book was published. The author describes the people, events and collections that contributed to the development of the Museum of Modern Art.

The Museum: 199 Years and the Metropolitan Museum of Art by Leo Lerman, published in 1969, chronicles the story of the Metropolitan Museum from its opening in 1869. Many original photographs and drawings enhance the text. Included is background on acquisitions, exhibits, museum administrators and cultural history.

Metropolitan Museum of Art publishes a quarterly Bulletin. The City Hall Library holds issues from 1969 to present. Articles on exhibits and collections by museum staff and art experts are featured. This publication provides a fascinating overview of the many and varied kinds of art that are available to the Museum visitor.

American Museum of Natural History Annual Report offers financial statistics, information on programs and exhibits, highlights of the past year and other facts. Holdings cover 1877 to 2003.

A New Brooklyn Museum: the Master Plan Competition, published in 1986, features proposed designs for a renovation and refurbishment of the museum An open competition was held. Master plans from the five finalists are reproduced with original charts, photographs, drawings and explanatory text. The winner was Arata Isozaki and Associates.

Vertical files are worth exploring for articles and other clippings on museums in general and particular. Find out more about various museums and how they contribute to the rich cultural life of the city.
