SPOTLIGHT ON: CONEY ISLAND HISTORY

By Christine Bruzzese

Home of the Cyclone, Steeplechase Park and the Nathan's hot dog, Coney Island in Brooklyn has been a seaside attraction since the late nineteenth century. In honor of another summer at the beach and the seventieth anniversary of the Cyclone roller coaster, this article will feature publications devoted to the subject of Coney Island.

The Improvement of Coney Island, Rockaway and South Beaches was published in 1939 by the Department of Parks. This report discusses problems with these beach areas and what actions can be taken to improve conditions. Issues such as overcrowded beaches, inadequate sanitary facilities and limited public transportation access are considered.

Good Old Coney Island: a Sentimental Journey into the Past by Edo McCullough was originally published in 1957. This is a chronicle of the attractions and distractions of Coney Island, written by someone whose family developed the area as a beachfront resort. Here are the stories of Steeplechase and Luna Park, the carnival sideshows, the food and the personalities that made Coney Island unique.

Sodom by the Sea: an Affectionate History of Coney Island by Oliver Pilat and Jo Ranson has a publication date of 1941. The time period covered is from the Dutch settlement until early 1941. This book chronicles the early settlement of the area, political leadership, stories of amusement parks and side shows, food concessions and more.

Consult Landmark Designation Reports prepared by the Landmarks Preservation Commission for information on the Cyclone, Parachute Jump and other landmarks at Coney Island. Also, explore the neighborhood files to learn more about the Coney Island area and surrounding communities.
