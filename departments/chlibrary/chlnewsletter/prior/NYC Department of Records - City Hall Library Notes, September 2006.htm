SPOTLIGHT ON: NYC HUMAN RESOURCES ADMINISTRATION

Human Resources Administration (HRA) was established by Mayor John Lindsay's Executive Order No. 28 dated August 15, 1966. The agency oversees various social services and programs that assist New Yorkers in need of them. Here is an overview of some of the publications of HRA that can be found in the City Hall Library collection.

Annual Reports from HRA were published in 1969, 1972, 1976 and 1987. This publication contains information on various programs and accomplishments of the agency along with statistics. Commissioner's Special Report, published in April 2005, discusses activities and achievements of HRA for 2004-2005 and offers various statistics. Future goals and objectives are also considered. A Time of Examination, a Time of Discovery, a Time of Accomplishment: January 2002 - June 2003 is a similar publication reviewing HRA programs and accomplishments.

District Resource Statement is an annual compilation of data prepared for the city's community boards by various city agencies and coordinated by the Office of Management and Budget. The report for HRA presents information on services offered directly or through contracted agencies in each community district. Included is statistical information on population, household income, and number of recipients for such programs as public assistance, Medicaid and food stamps. Also, there are listings of both HRA offices and outside contractors that provide services to the community districts. You can find copies of this publication dating back to the first issue in 1982, in the City Hall Library.

Over the years, HRA research analysts have prepared reports on such topics as Medicaid expenditures and recipient population, food stamp programs, public assistance recipients and the cost of welfare, and HIV/AIDS services. For example, Dependency, published semi-annually from 1984 to 1988, is a report on both poverty and social service programs offered by HRA which includes numerous statistical tables and charts. Low-Income Populations in New York City: Economic Trends and Social Welfare Programs, published annually from 1994 to 1996 by HRA and United Way of New York, was the successor publication.

Welfare Reform in Motion examines the recent changes made in public assistance programs by HRA/ Department of Social Services (DSS). Discussed are reworking of service delivery, restructuring and modernizing DSS operations, and programs to encourage self-sufficiency in welfare recipients among other issues. Future goals and objectives are examined, too.

Find out more about the history of HRA by using the vertical files. Among the materials to be found are organizational charts, booklets and news articles. Biographical files offer articles and other clippings on HRA Commissioners

Also of interest are reports published by predecessor agencies such as Department of Public Charities and Department of Welfare. Annual Reports and other publications contain useful historical information and statistics from the nineteenth century to the 1960's. Explore the resources described here and numerous others in the rich and varied collection of the City Hall Library in order to learn more about the Human Resources Administration and the history of social services in New York City.
